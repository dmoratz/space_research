About the Author








Arkady (1925–91) and Boris (1933–2012) Strugatsky were raised in Leningrad, the sons of an art critic and a teacher. The family was split up during the Siege of Leningrad, with Boris and his mother staying in the city, and Arkady and his father attempting to evacuate. The train they left on was bombed, with seventeen-year-old Arkady the only sur-vivor in their carriage. He was subsequently drafted into the Soviet army, and later became an editor and translator from English and Japanese. Boris, meanwhile, became an astronomer and engineer. In the mid-fifties, the brothers joined forces and began writing fiction together, a collabo-ration that would last until Arkady’s death in 1991. They wrote twenty-five novels, including Roadside Picnic, which was the basis for Andrei Tarkovsky’s classic film Stalker, as well as many short stories, essays, plays and film scripts. The asteroid 3054 Strugatskia is named after the brothers, who are regarded as Soviet Russia’s greatest science fiction writers.

HE LINGERED IN his sleep, not alive and yet not dead. Occasionally, his eyelids would open, his eyes moving wildly. To the people around him, it seemed as though he were following the ﬂight of some invisible fairy, his eyes now windows open to illuminate the darkness within his head, as if desperately hoping to light the way out from the blackness that had swallowed him up. He personally wasn’t conscious of this movement. Indeed, he had no feeling of anything in his entire body. When his eyes moved, the image in his mind was of the moment he was ejected from his beloved plane and left behind. His beloved plane, Yukikaze. As she ﬂew out of sight, leaving him with nothing but empty sky, he felt his existence contracting to a point before ﬁnally winking out. Only then did his eyes cease their wild motion, and his consciousness fell back, once again,
into the gap between life and death.
He was concerned with neither life nor death, simply letting time drift past, and even time itself no longer held any meaning for him.
Until, at last, a voice called for him to awaken.
